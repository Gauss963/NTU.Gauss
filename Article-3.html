Personal Note

November Sky

Posted on 2024-12-01 · Gauss Te-Chuan Chang

November began with an endless Ising Model simulation. Then everyone started arguing about that overpass. I don't know how others feel about it, but I really hate that overpass. I dislike how it clamps down on the skyline of Xinsheng South Rd. and cuts off Heping East Rd. In the following muddled days, I discovered that several of my Medium articles have been indexed by Google, which has greatly helped my views and readership. However, my next article has been stalled for quite a while; I hope to finish it before the end of the year. Before I knew it, it was already mid-month, and there was only a week left before going to Hengchun. Actually, I've long known that I know nothing about rocks — to the extent that I might get scammed when decorating a house in the future. After taking group photos for both departments, enjoying a free meal with Dr. Ma, and mixing a few beaker of chemicals, I boarded a bus to the south for some fun (at least I thought it would be fun). Hengchun was great. Before taking the course, I asked two friends who had taken the geological survey whether I should pay for it. The first person said, “Based on what I know about you, you'll be miserable.” The second person said, “That course is super fun; you'll definitely enjoy it.” Who was right? On the bus during return trip, I wrote down my thoughts like this:

"Overall, taking ‘Introduction to Field Geology (Ⅱ)’ this time was like taking a big bite of a watermelon. Before biting, I was excited; as I bit into it, the sweetness filled my mouth — extremely delightful — and then I bit into a seed. A burst of bitterness exploded in my mouth… And the second and third bites were the same. Can’t say it wasn’t tasty, but it would have been better if I could have removed those seeds."

After some time, when I’m no longer angry about the hole photographing event, I should post my full reflections on the course on Medium. At least before next semester starts? Might as well grab some traffic first.
